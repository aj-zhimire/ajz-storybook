Celebrating the remarkable life, groundbreaking work, and enduring wisdom of a woman who changed how we see ourselves and our relationship with the natural world.

Revolutionary Life

At 26, with no formal scientific training, Jane Goodall ventured into what is now Gombe Stream National Park in Tanzania. Her patient observations of chimpanzees revolutionized our understanding of what it means to be human. She discovered that chimpanzees make and use tools, have complex social relationships, and display emotions‚Äîfindings that redefined humanity itself.

Living Legacy

Today, at 90+, Dr. Goodall continues her mission through the Jane Goodall Institute and Roots & Shoots program, inspiring millions of young people worldwide to take action for animals, the environment, and their communities. Her message remains clear: each of us can make a difference.
